<p><p>The almond flour market is primarily categorized into three types: natural almond flour, blanched almond flour, and others. Natural almond flour retains the almond skins, resulting in a slightly coarser texture and a nuttier flavor, making it suitable for rustic recipes. Blanched almond flour, on the other hand, is made from peeled almonds, offering a finer texture and lighter color, ideal for baking. The 'others' category may include flavored or fortified almond flours, catering to specific dietary preferences.</p></p>
