<p><p>The flavor system market comprises three main types: natural flavors, artificial flavors, and nature-identical flavors. Natural flavors are derived from plant or animal sources and retain their original composition. Artificial flavors are synthetically created to mimic natural tastes, often cost-effective and versatile. Nature-identical flavors, while chemically identical to their natural counterparts, are produced artificially but maintain the same flavor profile. Each type serves diverse industries, including food and beverages, with specific applications based on cost, consumer preference, and regulatory standards.</p></p>
